# Movement
**Own your stage.**
Built by artists. Owned by artists. Powered by all of us.

*Technology killed the music scene. Movement is how we bring it back.*

---

## THE PROBLEM

**MASS STREAMING SERVICES — Own the digital**
Artists with millions of streams earn less than minimum wage. Tracks under 1,000 plays earn zero. The middle class of music is being deliberately de-monetized to fund the top 1%. Spotify does not have a relationship with your fans. It has a relationship with your data.

**LIVE NATION · TICKETMASTER — Own the physical**
A federal jury found Live Nation guilty of illegal monopoly on April 16, 2026. They control 86% of major venues. Artists and fans are squeezed from both ends simultaneously. The verdict doesn't fix it. The structure is still intact.

**THE GAP NOBODY HAS CLOSED**
Both platforms own the audience. The artist owns nothing.
The connection that happens in a live room — when a crowd turns, when people stop talking and start singing back — disappears the second the night ends. Technology captured the music but lost the moment. Movement gives that moment somewhere to live. And this time, the artist owns it.

---

## THE PLATFORM

**THE STAGE — Discovery. The scene.**
The homepage is not a magazine. It is a living room where something is always happening. Artists are surfaced by fan momentum, not editorial curation or algorithmic luck. Three tiers — up and coming, ready to break, established — determined entirely by audience behavior. Filterable by genre. No platform taste. No gatekeeping. The scene decides who rises.

**THE VAULT — Your archive. Your terms.**
Behind the subscription wall lives everything intimate. Writing sessions. Unreleased demos. Early access before the platforms get it. Subscriber-only chat during live streams. Direct conversations with the band. The Vault is not a content library. It is a backstage pass that never expires. Artists set their own price. Movement sets nothing.

**THE COLLECTIVE — The scene is back.**
Established artists can found their own named Label on Movement. They invite artists they believe in. Their name goes on that artist's page permanently. This is not a charity program. It is the digital version of what Jimi Hendrix did when he pulled an unknown band onto his stage — except now it has a financial mechanism underneath it. The established artist gains status and scene influence. The emerging artist gets visibility, resources, and a co-sign that means something. Success breeds success instead of concentrating at the top.

**THE FUND — The economy that runs underneath everything.**
Movement has its own currency: Hype Coins. Fans earn them by doing what fans have always done — spreading the word. Every share of a clip, every referral link that drives a new fan to an artist page, earns coins. Those coins can be spent to subscribe to artists when money isn't available. The broke 19-year-old who tells ten friends about a band is worth more to that band's future than a passive subscriber who never talks about them. The Fund makes that true in economic terms. Every coin earned is tracked back to the fan. Every coin spent flows as real subscription revenue to the artist. The platform converts it. The artist gets paid. The fan gets in.

## THE MECHANICS THAT MAKE THIS DIFFERENT

**The Claim**
Every fan gets one claim per artist. Not a follow. Not a like. A claim — a public declaration that says: I back this artist. Claim counts drive tier placement. The audience decides who rises. No algorithm. No platform curation. The scene operates the way it always has, finally given a digital layer.

**The Referral**
Every fan has a unique share link for every artist they follow. Every click they drive back to that artist's page is tracked and attributed. An artist can see exactly who their most valuable promoters are. Fans who hit referral thresholds unlock subscriber-tier content without paying cash — they paid in hype instead. This is not a growth hack. This is affiliate marketing built for people who love music.

**The Tier System**
- Lots of claims, short time on platform: **Up & coming**
- Claims accelerating, momentum building: **Ready to break**
- High claim count sustained over time: **Established**

An algorithm without being an algorithm. The audience drives it. The platform just makes it visible.

**The 15-Second Share**
When something real happens on Stage — a song lands, an artist says something true, a writing session produces a moment — fans capture it. Fifteen seconds. Shared anywhere. No Movement watermark. No "shared from." Just the artist, the moment, and a link back to their discovery page. The fan becomes the ambassador. The clip doesn't promote the platform. It promotes the artist. Movement is invisible. The artist is the story.

## WHO GETS VALUE

**The established artist**
Direct revenue. Fan relationships they actually own and can export. Status as a scene-maker through The Collective. A platform that works in their interest — structurally, not just rhetorically.

**The emerging artist**
A path forward that doesn't require algorithmic luck or a label deal. Co-signs from artists they respect. Access to established audiences through The Collective. A place to build from the first ten fans with real economic tools from day one.

**The fan who can pay**
Proximity to artists they love. Discovery through trust, not algorithms. The feeling of "I found this band" — and a frictionless way to prove it to everyone they know.

**The fan who can't pay**
Hype Coins. Share a clip, drive a fan, earn your way in. The platform does not gate-keep on income. It rewards effort and belief. The most broke fan might be the most valuable one.

**Revenue streams:**
Artist subscriptions (fan-to-artist, artist sets price) · Hype Coin conversions · Pay-per-view moments · Tips and live donations · Concert ticket sales direct through platform · Merch drops · The Collective Fund (milestone-triggered, artist-directed)

**The Hype Coins model:**
Fans earn coins through sharing and referral activity. Coins are spent on artist subscriptions. The platform converts coins to real subscription revenue and pays artists at the same 80% rate. The coin economy circulates value inside the ecosystem instead of extracting it out.
